Trash that swallowed the sea

Plastic, plastic, everywhere

In the ocean, plastic debris injures and kills fish, seabirds and marine mammals. Marine plastic pollution has impacted at least 267 species worldwide, including 86% of all sea turtle species, 44% of all seabird species and 43% of all marine mammal species. The impacts include fatalities as a result of ingestion, starvation, suffocation, infection, drowning, and entanglement.

Most marine debris (80%) comes from trash and debris in urban runoff, i.e. land-based sources. Key components of land-based sources include litter, trash and debris from construction, ports and marinas, commercial and industrial facilities, and trash blown out of garbage containers, trucks, and landfills.1 Ocean-based sources, such as, overboard discharges from ships and discarded fishing gear, account for the other 20%.

In 2010, a California grey whale washed up dead on the shores of the Puget Sound. Autopsies indicated that its stomach contained a pair of pants and a golf ball, more than 20 plastic bags, small towels, duct tape and surgical gloves.

Stack and Stack

Sustainable, more beautiful design

High-concept furniture designers, are getting in on the action, finding innovative ways to make products that are not only good for the environment, but look great, too. Upcycling ocean plastic and rethinking post-consumer waste are two of the most popular ways that these creative minds are embracing sustainability in order to enable a more circular economy.

Alas, right now, most of these pieces are major-splurge, made-to-order situations. The ultimate goal, of course, is to live in a world where responsible design is not atypical, but the everyday norm. Until then, here's a roundup of the coolest sustainable high-concept furniture to keep you both creatively inspired and in touch with the latest environmental efforts out there.
